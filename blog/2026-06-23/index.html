Griffey Card Market Report — June 23, 2026

What moved in the Ken Griffey Jr. card market on June 23, 2026, based on real eBay sold listings across 10 years.

1990

Toys R Us Rookies #13 climbing — PSA 8 moved from $23 to $30 and PSA 9 from $44 to $60. Donruss #365 PSA 10 ticked up from $120 to $131 at auction with 20 bids — condition-sensitive cards are rewarding patience. Topps #336 PSA 8 rebounded from $11 to $24 while PSA 9 softened from $45 to $36 — buyers shifting to the more affordable grade. Added Bowman Tiffany PSA 9 at $150.

1991

Desert Shield All-Star #392 PSA 8 continues climbing — now at $600, up from $575 two days ago. Quiet strength across 1991 Score inserts: All-Star PSA 9 up to $30, Dream Team to $25, Rifleman to $17. Topps #790 PSA 9 jumped from $18 to $25. The junk wax base is finding a floor and bouncing.

1992

Split market in '92 today. Upper Deck #424 PSA 10 nearly doubled from $105 to $199. Leaf #392 PSA 10 surged from $99 to $150 — those tough black borders make gems scarce. Bowman #100 PSA 9 jumped from $22 to $40. But inserts cooled: Score All-Star PSA 10 dropped hard from $270 to $140 at auction with 19 bids, and Studio #232 PSA 9 fell from $60 to $49. Gem mint base climbing while mid-grade inserts give back.

1993

The one we've been waiting for. 1993 Finest Refractor #110 PSA 9 sold for $13,600 at auction with 47 bids — the original Finest Refractor Griffey, and now the second most expensive card in the entire guide. Only the 1998 Crusade Red sits higher. This is THE card that launched the refractor era. Upper Deck #355 PSA 10 bounced back from $61 to $70.

1994

SP #105 PSA 10 dropped from $200 to $158 at auction with 33 bids — strong data pulling it down. Leaf Power Brokers PSA 10 also softened from $173 to $158. Premium base cards in '94 are cooling while the chase inserts (Finest Refractor, Flair Hot Gloves) hold firm.

1995

Pinnacle Bubble Gum #128 gave back yesterday's gains — PSA 9 settled from $258 back to $240 with multiple sales in the $230-$250 range, and PSA 8 dipped from $118 to $114. The iconic card is finding its level. Flair Outfield Power PSA 9 held at $61, confirming yesterday's move.

1996

Metal Universe Platinum Edition PSA 8 more than doubled from $130 to $300 — the parallel premium on this card is real. Finest Gold Intimidators PSA 9 climbed from $355 to $400. Flair Diamond Cuts PSA 9 surged from $210 to $300. The high-end 1996 inserts are on fire right now — anything with scarcity is being chased hard.

1997

Metal Universe Blast Furnace PSA 10 jumped from $350 to $585 — a 67% move at 1:48 Hobby odds. Metal Universe inserts across '97 are heating up fast. Ultra Power Plus PSA 9 sold for $1,009 at auction (29 bids, POP 3) — a card we hadn't even been tracking. The depth of demand in '97 keeps surprising.

1998

Donruss Crusade Red (/25) PSA 8 sold for $32,433 at auction with 52 bids. That's now the most expensive card in this entire guide — 25 copies exist on earth. Added to the database today. All-Galactic Team PSA 8 surged again from $830 to $1,500 — this card has moved from $750 to $1,500 in three days. Diamond Heroes PSA 9 up from $375 to $450. Fleer Tradition Zone PSA 8 up from $250 to $288. 1998 Metal Universe is the hottest corner of the Griffey market right now.

1999

PSA 8 prices climbing across the board. Planet Metal PSA 8 moved from $256 to $340. Lords of the Diamond Refractor PSA 8 jumped from $240 to $359. Black Diamond Single PSA 10 nearly doubled from $109 to $204. Triumvirate Luminous #T13B PSA 8 up from $62 to $91. Added Finest Team Finest Gold (/250) PSA 9 at $600 and Finest Double Feature Refractor (w/ A-Rod) PSA 10 at $175. The PSA 8 grade is where the action is in '99 right now.
