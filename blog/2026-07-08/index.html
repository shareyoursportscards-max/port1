Griffey Card Market Report — July 8, 2026

What moved in the Ken Griffey Jr. card market on July 8, 2026, based on real eBay sold listings across 6 years.

1994

The Flair Hot Gloves #3 just made its statement in gem: PSA 10 jumped from $750 to $1,200, the third grade on this die-cut to reprice upward in as many weeks. The whole 1994 premium tier followed — Finest Base #232 PSA 10 climbed to $550 while five raw sales in four days confirmed the $31 floor, and the Stadium Club Finest #5 PSA 9 pushed from $52 to $84 on a 21-bid auction. The sneaky mover was the Select Crown Contenders, whose raw price nearly quintupled from $16 to $78 — a card that was an afterthought a month ago. One head-scratcher for the watchlist: two SP Holoview Blue PSA 9s sold the same day for $160 and $35. Until the market picks a lane, the guide holds at $100.

1995

Eleven separate Bubble Gum sales in four days — the most traded Griffey card in the hobby did its thing again, and this time every grade moved. Raw climbed from $50 to $65 on six sales, PSA 8 jumped from $109 to $155 at auction, and PSA 9 settled at $265 across four sales clustered within $25 of each other. Even the Museum Collection parallel got its first raw price on the books at $375. Beyond the gum: the Finest Base #118 finally showed its PSA 10 hand at $850 — a POP 17 card that had been a blank forever — and the Flair Base #81 PSA 10 rose to $310 while its PSA 9 doubled to $65. The corrections: Flair Hot Numbers PSA 9 eased from $200 to $149, and the Topps #397 PSA 10 slid from $196 to $140 on back-to-back sales.

1996

The story of the week in 1996 is the Metal Universe Platinum Edition collapsing under its own hype — four raw sales in four days, all in the $74-$150 range, dragging the book value down from $330 to $81, with PSA 8 following it down from $400 to $150. That's not a correction, that's a card getting fully repriced after someone found a stash. Elsewhere the story is the opposite: the Flair Diamond Cuts #5 PSA 10 pushed to $2,127, and the Leaf Limited Lumberjacks #1 PSA 10 jumped from $325 to $500 — both cards quietly climbing while nobody was watching Metal Universe implode. The Finest Sterling Refractor #24 raw doubled to $400 on back-to-back sales, and the Topps Chrome #70 PSA 9 finally cracked triple digits at $100. A reminder that in a market this size, the biggest mover and the best mover are rarely the same card.

1997

The Bowman Chrome Refractor #12 PSA 9 nearly doubled overnight, jumping from $536 to $949 on a clean fixed-price sale — one of the biggest single-day moves on a graded '97 refractor in weeks. The Flair Showcase Diamond Cuts #5 is having a moment across every grade: raw held at $475, but PSA 8 climbed to $510 and PSA 9 surged from $525 to $750. The Leaf Dress for Success /3,500 split down the middle — PSA 9 up to $200, but PSA 10 gave back ground from $717 to $675 on a lower confirmed sale. And Flair Showcase Row 0 raw corrected hard from $640 to $425 — a healthy pullback after weeks of climbing without a real ceiling test. The '97 Bowman's Best International Atomic Refractor also ticked up, PSA 8 now at $609.

1998

The Stadium Club Triumvirate Luminescent had been sitting on a blank PSA 9 for months — a real sale finally filled it in, and it filled in loud: $1,780, more than double what the raw and PSA 8 numbers would have predicted. That's the kind of gap that happens when a card almost never gets graded. The Ultra Win Now Acetate got the same treatment on PSA 9, landing at $300 for the first time. On the correction side, the Fleer Tradition Zone #7 raw eased from $240 to $203, the Finest Centurion raw continued settling to $165, and Metal Universe Diamond Heroes raw slipped to $75 — three cards finding their real level after being propped up on thin data. The Leaf Fractal Materials /250 raw ticked up to $207, and Metal Universe's Universal Language insert notched a new PSA 10 high at $300.

1999

The Skybox Thunder Dial 1 #3D PSA 10 doubled outright — $1,350 to $2,700 — on a card with 1:300 odds that almost never trades in gem. The Topps Chrome All-Etch Refractor #AE3 raw kept climbing too, up to $545, and the Skybox Molten Metal Xplosion #133 PSA 10 nearly tripled from $185 to $522. But the cleanest story of the day is a correction: the Skybox Thunder Turbocharged #3 raw had been parked at an absurd $1,524 — higher than its own PSA 9 and PSA 10 — clearly stale data. A real sale at $139.99 reset it to $140, which finally makes sense next to the card's other grades. The Flair Showcase Measure of Greatness #4 /500 filled in its raw price at $400 and PSA 8 jumped to $490, and the UD View to a Thrill Double PSA 9 nearly tripled from $77 to $219. Busy week for a set that keeps surprising.
