Griffey Card Market Report — July 9, 2026

What moved in the Ken Griffey Jr. card market on July 9, 2026, based on real eBay sold listings across 5 years.

1990

The Topps #336 PSA 10 pushed from $1,100 to $1,365 at auction with 18 bids — the strongest sale on the flagship rookie in months. Glossy Rookies #11 PSA 10 jumped from $380 to $650 on back-to-back sales at $725 and $633. Donruss Best of the AL PSA 9 nearly doubled from $51 to $99, Bowman Tiffany PSA 10 rose from $466 to $500, Diamond Kings PSA 10 moved from $860 to $900, and Topps Tiffany PSA 9 climbed from $380 to $400. The base tier cooled underneath: Donruss #365 PSA 10 fell from $203 to $145 on two sales, O-Pee-Chee #336 PSA 9 slid from $148 to $120, Major League Debut #46 PSA 10 dropped from $270 to $228 at auction a day after a $307 sale, and Upper Deck #156 PSA 10 eased from $140 to $131. The Toys R Us Rookies #13 PSA 10 sold at exactly $510 again — three confirmations in ten days.

1991

Desert Shield day. The #790 PSA 10 hit $4,500, the PSA 8 eased to $999, and the Desert Shield All-Star #392 PSA 9 climbed to $900 — military-only distribution keeps supply low. First PSA 10 data on the O-Pee-Chee #790 landed at $483 at auction with 30 bids, and the O-Pee-Chee Premier #56 PSA 10 jumped from $144 to $200. Underneath, the whole '91 tier moved up on July 7-8 results: Topps #790 PSA 10 from $150 to $156, Score All-Star #396 from $130 to $136, Dream Team #892 from $95 to $115 on three sales, Donruss #77 from $92 to $103, Leaf #372 from $95 to $100 with 19 bids, and Donruss All-Stars #49 from $80 to $100.

1992

The Donruss Elite #13 showed its whole ladder in one week: a PSA 10 sold for $5,226 at auction with 45 bids, a PSA 9 brought $822, and even a PSA 7 cleared $516. One of 10,000 printed in an era when serial numbers barely existed, and gem copies almost never surface. Studio #232 rose across three grades — PSA 10 from $410 to $455 at auction, PSA 9 from $49 to $77, PSA 8 from $25 to $40. Upper Deck Diamond Skills #650 PSA 10 climbed from $45 to $60. On the other side, Stadium Club Members Choice #603 PSA 10 slipped from $399 to $375 and Ultra #123 PSA 10 gave back from $77 to $70.

1993

First PSA 10 price on the Donruss Diamond Kings #DK-1: $1,500 — a low-pop gem on a card everybody owned raw. Stadium Club Members Choice #591 PSA 10 also got its first number, $148 on a pop-17 card. The Finest #110 keeps building: PSA 9 from $221 to $240 on two same-day sales and raw copies from $66 up to $90. Topps #179 PSA 10 rose from $65 to $90, and Diamond Kings PSA 8 ticked up from $27 to $36. SP Platinum Power #PP9 PSA 9 pulled back from $200 to $158 at auction, and Black Gold #33 PSA 9 gave back its June jump, falling from $60 to $35.

1994

Steady day in '94 — sale after sale confirmed existing prices: Flair Hot Gloves PSA 10 at exactly $1,200, Finest #232 PSA 10 at $550, Leaf Statistical Standouts PSA 10 at $480, Stadium Club Finest #5 PSA 9 at $84 with 21 bids, plus a stack of raw confirmations. SP #105 PSA 10 bounced back from $158 to $200 after its late-June dip. Select Crown Contenders raw gave back its spike, falling from $78 to $38 at auction with 14 bids. First PSA 8 data on the Leaf Limited Gold All-Stars /10,000 landed at $40.
