Griffey Card Market Report — July 10, 2026

What moved in the Ken Griffey Jr. card market on July 10, 2026, based on real eBay sold listings across 9 years. Price moves are sale to sale, not averages.

1990

Raw cards led the day. A Topps #336 sold ungraded for $40 at auction — above the $33-$34 that two PSA 9 copies brought the same day, which pulled the PSA 9 from $40 to $34. The Topps Tiffany #336 raw more than doubled from $90 to $201. Upper Deck #156 PSA 10 bounced from $131 to $150 a day after its last sale, while Donruss #365 PSA 10 eased from $145 to $138 at auction with 25 bids. Major League Debut #46 split: PSA 8 up from $20 to $35, PSA 9 down from $42 to $38. First raw prices landed on Diamond Kings #4 at $50 and Best of the AL #1 at $45, and a $1,350 sale backed up yesterday's $1,365 on the Topps #336 PSA 10.

1991

The Stadium Club #270 PSA 10 broke out: a 27-bid auction hit $477 and a $500 sale followed the next day, moving the card from $400 to $490. The PSA 9 went with it, from $30 to $50. Bowman #246 PSA 10 jumped from $85 to $150, and Topps All-Star #392 PSA 10 edged from $70 to $78 on three sales. First PSA 10 prices: Ultra Gold at $286 and Score Rifleman #697 at $85. Desert Shield held its marks — the #790 PSA 8 sold at $999 and the All-Star #392 PSA 9 at $900.

1992

Donruss Elite #13 keeps climbing — a PSA 9 sold for $999.99 two days after the PSA 10's $5,226 auction, moving the grade from $822 to $1,000. Junk wax raw showed life underneath: first raw prices on Pinnacle #549 at $24 and Topps #50 at $19, and Studio #232 raw up from $7 to $13. Donruss #165 PSA 10 rose from $45 to $60 and Bloodlines #85 PSA 8 from $12 to $19. Stadium Club Members Choice #603 PSA 9 gave back its recent gain, falling from $67 to $36 at auction, and Topps #50 PSA 9 slipped from $18 to $15 on two sales.

1993

Finest #110 traded heavily — five sales across grades in four days. First PSA 8 data landed at $113 in a 19-bid auction, the PSA 9 confirmed at $240 twice, and three raw sales between $82 and $94 held raw copies at $90. Diamond Kings #DK-1 raw nearly tripled from $10 to $29. Leaf #319 cooled at the top: PSA 10 from $125 to $99 and PSA 9 from $28 to $25.

1994

Stadium Club Finest #5 raw more than doubled from $13 to $31, led by a 22-bid auction at $36.55. Pinnacle The Naturals #3 raw moved from $17 to $25 on three same-day sales, and Leaf Power Brokers #5 PSA 9 climbed from $25 to $29 at auction. SP #105 was mixed — the PSA 10 confirmed at $200, first PSA 8 data arrived at $22, and the PSA 9 eased from $40 to $35 on a second same-day sale. Dugout Dirt #7 PSA 10 slipped from $125 to $105. First raw prices: SP Holoview FX #12 at $21 and Mickey Mantle's Long Shots #MM10 at $20.

1996

A 41-bid auction sent the Finest Refractor #135 PSA 8 from $560 to $1,250 — more than PSA 9 copies have been bringing. First PSA 10 data on the You Make the Play Gold Signature #16 landed at $1,335, and the Stadium Club Extreme Winners Silver #EW8 PSA 9 debuted at $195. The raw insert market was broadly up: Finest Refractor #305 from $250 to $366, Ultra Diamond Producers Gold Medallion doubled from $50 to $100, Metal Universe Platinum #107 from $81 to $108, Mystery Finest Refractor #M16 from $45 to $73, and the Leaf Limited Pennant Craze and Lumberjacks from $42 to $72 and $70, with Mother Lode, Stained Glass, Select Certified Red and Fleer Zone all up behind them. Graded strength too: Sterling Refractor #24 PSA 9 from $536 to $625, Starburst Artist's Proof PSA 9 from $220 to $333, and Metal Universe #107 PSA 8 from $90 to $150. Two gave back: Ultra Thunder Clap #11 raw from $1,000 to $900 and Fleer Update Diamond Tribute #3 raw from $159 to $104.

1997

The Finest Gold Refractor #342 got its first PSA 8 price: $1,050. The rest of Finest moved with it — Refractor #139 PSA 9 from $725 to $795, base #139 PSA 10 from $202 to $225. A raw Metal Universe Magnetic Field sold for $400, up from $79 — PSA 9 money for an ungraded copy. Fleer Bleacher Blasters raw climbed from $155 to $186 in a 21-bid auction, Stadium Club Firebrand #FB5 raw from $115 to $160 with first PSA 8 data at $125, Studio Hard Hats raw from $80 to $123, and Leaf Dress for Success raw from $72 to $90. First prices also landed on Topps Hobby Masters #HM1 PSA 9 at $190 and the Leaf Fractal Matrix Z-Axis Die-Cut /100 raw at $176. The other direction: Ultra Baseball Rules! #2 raw from $136 to $80, Donruss Preferred X-Ponential Power raw from $150 to $99, Studio Press Proof Gold #16 raw from $147 to $93, and SPx Cornerstones of the Game raw from $198 to $172.

1998

A 27-bid auction moved the Finest Non-Protected Refractor PSA 9 from $230 to $609 — closing the gap on its PSA 10 at $1,150. E-X2001 #10 cooled at both ends: PSA 9 from $355 to $338 in a 30-bid auction, raw from $73 to $38 on back-to-back auctions. Zenith Z-Team Gold got its first raw price at $73 after an 18-bid auction, and Metal Universe Hardball Galaxy its first PSA 8 at $50. Finest Centurion /500 raw ticked up from $165 to $170, while Topps Lords of the Diamond raw slipped from $40 to $35 and Pinnacle Epix raw from $63 to $28.

1999

Team Finest Gold /250 got its first raw price at $500. The E-X Century #17 PSA 9 jumped from $100 to $225 — with a single PSA 10 in the pop report, the 9 is the top of this card's ladder. Triumvirate was strong across tiers: first raw data on the Illuminator #T13B at $352 after a 20-bid auction, and Luminous #T3B up at both ends — PSA 8 from $65 to $71, raw from $44 to $62. Stadium Club #200 PSA 10 rose from $100 to $135, Standing Ovation #81 PSA 8 from $129 to $160, Damage Inc. #8 raw from $40 to $55, and UD Choice StarQuest Red raw doubled from $25 to $52 in a 17-bid auction. Topps Chrome Lords of the Diamond Refractor raw came back to earth, from $425 to $286 on two sales, and Finest Prominent Figures /457 raw debuted at $128.
