About these numbers. Values shown are general market estimates compiled from typical recent sale ranges for these issues — not a live price feed. 1990s "junk wax era" base cards were printed in massive quantities, so raw and PSA 8 copies are usually inexpensive while PSA 10 gem copies (especially on the era's notoriously hard-to-center brands like Donruss and Score) carry steep premiums due to scarcity in top grade. Before buying or selling, check current sold listings on eBay, PSA's price guide, 130point.com, or Card Ladder. N/A means that grade is rarely seen or not applicable for that card. Pack Odds are the manufacturer's stated insertion ratio (e.g. "1:24" = one per 24 packs) and are shown only where I could verify them from set checklists; a dash means base cards (no odds apply) or odds I couldn't confirm. An asterisk (*) means the ratio varies by parallel tier within that insert and the exact tier for this card is unconfirmed.
